About

At the intersection of road safety engineering and human factors, I develop and evaluate evidence-based interventions that are both theoretically grounded and operationally effective.

Over the past 8 years, my work has focused on advancing road safety through research at the intersection of driving behaviour, human factors, and transportation infrastructure design. I am Harish Saini, PhD in Transportation Engineering from IIT Jammu, and my approach is grounded in rigorous empirical analysis, evidence-based decision-making, and practical relevance—with the goal of translating research into interventions that measurably improve safety and operational performance.

My research interests include behavioural modelling, risk assessment, and the evaluation of engineering and policy countermeasures for safer road environments. I’m particularly interested in bridging research and practice, ensuring that findings are actionable for stakeholders such as agencies, planners, and engineering teams.
